Kepler-186f:

Kepler-186f is an exoplanet that orbits the red dwarf star Kepler-186. It is about 580 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Cygnus. The planet is around 10% larger than Earth, making it one of the most Earth-sized planets discovered. It takes about 130 days to orbit its star and is located in the habitable zone, where temperatures could allow liquid water to exist. Scientists believe Kepler-186f is a rocky planet, but they do not know if it has an atmosphere or oceans because it is too far away to study in detail.

Kepler-186f was discovered in 2014 by NASA scientists using the Kepler Space Telescope. Scientists found it with the transit method, which detects tiny drops in a star's brightness when a planet passes in front of it. It was the first Earth-sized planet discovered in the habitable zone of another star, making it an important milestone in the search for planets that could support life.

Scientists believe Kepler-186f could have some of the conditions needed for life because it is rocky and orbits in the habitable zone. If it has an atmosphere and liquid water, it may be able to support simple forms of life. However, there is no evidence that life exists there, and its atmosphere has not yet been observed. More powerful telescopes in the future may help scientists learn whether Kepler-186f is truly habitable.
